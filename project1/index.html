Personal Statement Topic

For my personal statement, I wrote about my love for musicals and how my desire to be in the spotlight pushed me out of my comfort zone. I crafted an extended metaphor that likens Aaron Burr's journey in the musical Hamilton to my own. My journey mirrors the show's core theme: the relentless pursuit of leaving your mark and 'not throwing away your shot.' For years, I was Aaron Burr—'waiting for it,' observing from the wings, my ideas and voice perpetually interrupted by my own social anxiety. But I longed to be Hamilton, to step into the spotlight and fight for my place. My work as a tutor and a TA was my 'room where it happens'—the moment I stopped waiting and started building my own stage. I transformed my role as a spectator in the audience into one as a principle cast member who helps others learn their lines and find their spotlight.

Hook/Introduction of Personal Statement

I was a Burr my entire life. Not Washington, not Jefferson, nor Adams. I was the forgotten founding father of America who always hesitated. But behind the curtains, I longed to be Hamilton, the star of the show who wastes no time fighting for his ideals. Although I dreamed of becoming the protagonist of my story, I never dared to step onto the stage until I stumbled upon an illegal 2-hour-22-minute-long musical.
